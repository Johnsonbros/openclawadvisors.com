Why I Run 4 AI Agents as a Plumber

February 4, 2026 8 min read Story OpenClaw

The Problem Nobody Talks About

I co-own a plumbing company. Johnson Bros. Plumbing & Drain Cleaning, Greater Boston area. My brother Nick and I handle everything from clogged drains to boiler installs.

Here's what nobody tells you about running a service business: the work isn't the hard part. I can fix a frozen pipe. I can install a water heater. That's muscle memory at this point.

The hard part is everything around the work:

  • Answering calls while you're elbow-deep in someone's crawl space
  • Remembering which customer needs a follow-up
  • Researching that weird Viessmann boiler error code at 9 PM
  • Keeping track of scheduling across 50+ active jobs
  • Not dropping balls when your phone has 47 unread messages

That's the stuff that kills you. Not the plumbing.

What I've Learned

1. AI is leverage, not replacement.
My agents don't do plumbing. They do everything around plumbing ‚Äî the coordination, research, memory, and planning that would otherwise eat my evenings and weekends.

2. Start with one agent, add as you need.
I didn't build four agents on day one. I started with ZEKE. Then I realized I needed dedicated research (Huginn). Then memory management (Muninn). Then architecture (Odin). Each one solved a specific pain point.

3. The "blue collar + AI" gap is massive.
Almost nobody in the trades is doing this. The AI conversation is dominated by tech companies and knowledge workers. But service businesses ‚Äî plumbers, electricians, HVAC, landscapers ‚Äî have more to gain, because we're already stretched thin.

4. Open source matters.
I chose OpenClaw because it's open source. My data stays on my server. My agents work for me, not for some SaaS company. When something breaks, I can fix it. When I need a feature, I can build it.
